## What

A one-command way to detach a plugin from the lifecycle: drop `<name>` from
**every** hook key in the landing's `plugins.toml` in a single edit, the
parallel of the existing per-hook `bl conf remove <op>.<phase> <name>`.

## Why

Detaching `bl-delivery` today is **five** separate calls — `claim.post`,
`close.pre`, `close.post`, `unclaim.post`, `prime.post` (the read-op `show` can
stay: its only act prints a worktree line *"when the worktree actually exists"*,
a no-op outside a code repo). That is painful to invoke and, worse, ugly to
name in an error string: the sibling fix bl-4a88 (the non-git warning) wants to
point the user at "detach delivery for pure tracking" and shouldn't have to spell
five commands to do it.

This is also the **explicit declaration** of "I don't want this plugin" — the
durable *intent* that the rejected implicit-removal-at-prime would have *inferred*
from circumstance (see bl-4a88's decided-approach notes). The intent gets one
clean command; circumstance only ever earns a warning.

## Shape (decide spelling in review)

The mechanism already exists — `conf_write.rs` `hooks_edit` removes a name from
one key and drops the key if it goes empty. This just maps that over all hook
keys. Candidate spellings, pick the most minimal:

- `bl conf remove-plugin <name>` — new sub-verb, reads well, but grows the conf
  surface.
- `bl conf remove <name>` with no `op.phase` — bare name means "everywhere".
  No new verb, but overloads `remove`'s arity.

Prefer adding at most ONE affordance; don't grow `conf` further than this.

## Relationship

Independent of bl-4a88 (the warn fix works without this — it just spells the
five-call form). Once this lands, bl-4a88's warning message should point here
instead. Pure ergonomic over existing config; no new persisted state.
