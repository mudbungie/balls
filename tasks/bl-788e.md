WHY: §10's explicit-edge model is correct (the auto-minted edge was a gap factory) but fails
SILENT in the common case: --parent is the natural spelling and carries no edge, so a
forgotten --blocks close leaves an epic closeable over open children with no signal. The cost
is a standing human-advisory burden ("remember to wire dependencies") — the overhead the tool
exists to remove. Advice has demonstrably not fixed it; mechanism must.

UNTIE: make the ergonomic spelling carry the edge IN ITS NAME.
- --subtask-of E  ≡  --parent E --blocks close  ("part of E; E can't finish until I do").
  Pure front-door sugar over existing primitives — zero new core schema; §10 already states
  "the create flags are sugar over the general primitive, never a constraint on it".
  Mutually exclusive with --parent (it IS a parent spelling); composes with --needs.
- --parent stays the bare display pointer for genuinely-pure containment.
- --needs already names its intent; hold the line against further intent flags
  (--depends-on etc. — --needs covers it).

RESIDUE-CATCHER: close emits a NOTICE — the §12 "diagnostic, never authority" pattern —
"closing with N open children, none gating" (any live child at a successful close is
non-gating by definition; their parent: pointers now dangle, display-only). The child scan is
the one `show` already does for the tree. A notice, never a block.

Deliverable: spec §9/§10 + §15 entry; front-door parser + close notice + tests; SKILL.md
(teach --subtask-of as the default subtask spelling).
