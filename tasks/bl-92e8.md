Two related gaps between §4/§6 and the read path. (1) architecture.md §4:328-330: 'core narrates mutating-op lifecycle at info and read-op narration (show/list) at debug' — but src/reads.rs constructs no Log at all; at any --log-level a read emits zero records, and the read-dispatched plugin's stderr goes to Stdio::null (src/reads/readop.rs:84). (2) §6:516-518: 'Reads are not special-cased. Every op (incl. show/list) has a hook key' — but only Verb::Show calls readop::fold (src/reads.rs:151-168); a user-wired [hooks] "list" or "dep-tree" entry is silently never dispatched — config that validates and does nothing.

Fix either direction per key: implement debug-level narration + dispatch for list/dep-tree, or strike the spec claims via §15 ('only show dispatches; list/dep-tree are hook-free'). Silent-accept of a wired-but-dead hook key is the one shape that must not survive: if a key can't fire, wiring it should warn or error.

**Why:** a config surface that accepts wiring it will never run violates fail-loud and single-source-of-truth at once — the plugins.toml says one thing, dispatch does another, and nothing tells the user.
