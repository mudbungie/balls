End-to-end integration test for bl-chore (gated on the implementation).

Per the project convention, integration behavior that core's src/ coverage can't reach lives in tests/*.rs (coverage-neutral — only src/ counts toward 100%). Use that here to exercise the real wiring without fighting the coverage gate.

Cover:
- Wire bl-chore into claim.post on a throwaway repo (isolated XDG), claim a leaf task, assert the two configured chores are minted as `--blocks close` children carrying the tag, and that the parent then refuses `bl close` until they close.
- Recursion: claim one of the minted chores; assert tag-skip fires (no chore-of-a-chore).
- Epic-skip: claim a task that already has a child; assert nothing is minted (default knob on). Flip the knob; assert it does mint.
- Idempotency: unclaim → reclaim; assert no duplicate chores.
- Gate children don't clutter readiness (don't appear in `bl list` ready/blocked status).

Follow the E2E demo-artifact conventions (freshly-built binary + throwaway /tmp repo + isolated XDG).
